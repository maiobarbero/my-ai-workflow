# Management Approaches

Use detail and dynamic complexity to recommend a fit-for-purpose management
approach.

## Quadrant Logic

| Detail Complexity | Dynamic Complexity | Quadrant | Management Fit | Main Risk |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Low | Low | Simple / Internal | Keep simple | Over-managing |
| High | Low | Complicated | Systems management / control | Rigidity |
| Low | High | Complex | Interactive management / connecting | Endless discussion |
| High | High | Complex + complicated | Dynamic management | Poor balance between control and interaction |

Use "High" for scores 4-5, "Low" for scores 1-2, and judgment for score 3.
If score 3 is paired with high impact or uncertainty, treat it as leaning high.

## Internal / Keep Simple

Fits when:

- Detail complexity is low.
- Dynamic complexity is low.
- Stakeholders broadly agree.
- Problems can be isolated.

Use:

- Minimal governance.
- Clear owner.
- Simple milestones.
- Avoid process overhead.

Risk:

- Ignoring hidden external or organizational complexity.
- Informing stakeholders too late if complexity was underestimated.

## Systems Management / Control

Fits when:

- Detail complexity is high.
- Dynamic complexity is low.
- Work can be decomposed.
- Facts and goals are broadly shared.
- Interfaces can be specified.
- Decisions are predictable enough.

Use controlling interventions:

- Work Breakdown Structure.
- Interface register.
- Milestones and decision gates.
- Risk register.
- Requirements baseline.
- Schedule and cost control.
- Formal quality criteria.
- Contractual responsibilities.

Risk:

- Rigid control fails when actors, priorities, or scope change.
- Team may optimize technical content while missing external dynamics.

## Interactive Management / Connecting

Fits when:

- Detail complexity is low to medium.
- Dynamic complexity is high.
- Many players have different interests.
- Actors are autonomous.
- Hierarchy is incomplete.
- Problems are unstructured.
- Issues are interwoven.
- Decision process is unpredictable.

Use connecting interventions:

- Early alignment sessions.
- Shared framing of problem and value.
- Scenario exploration.
- Short feedback cycles.
- Transparent communication.
- Joint risk or opportunity workshops.
- Scope discussion before premature solution lock-in.

Risk:

- Endless discussion.
- False expectations.
- No clear decision authority.
- Consensus seeking blocks progress.

## Dynamic Management

Fits when:

- Detail complexity is high.
- Dynamic complexity is high.
- Project is both complicated and complex.
- Control and interaction are both necessary.

Use mixed interventions:

- Combine decomposition with stakeholder interaction.
- Use decision gates, but keep room for learning.
- Use interface control plus joint workshops.
- Maintain transparent issue escalation.
- Reassess complexity at phase changes.
- Balance prepare-and-commit with predict-and-control.

Risk:

- Too much control suppresses adaptation.
- Too much interaction weakens decisions.
- Leadership fails to balance both modes.

## Value Creation Lens

Complexity is not only a threat. Sometimes increasing complexity can create value
by reframing the project, adding useful scope, or involving actors who unlock new
solutions.

When recommending management fit, include one value opportunity if visible:

- Reframe the project goal.
- Turn stakeholder conflict into broader support.
- Use constraints to unlock alternative funding or scope.
- Convert interfaces into reusable assets.
- Use compliance or trust work to increase adoption.

Do not force a value opportunity if none is supported by the user's description.
